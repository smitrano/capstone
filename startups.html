For Startups

Tangible steps for tomorrow's business leaders.

Get Started

Your startup can be a force for good.

Take steps to better your interactions with workers, your community, and your environment.

Learn How

Workers

Wages

Pay a living wage to employees, including part time and temporary employees and independent contractors.

Engagement

Conduct regular, anonymous worker satisfaction and engagement surveys.

Regularly collect (and make transparent) data on employee metrics such as retention, turnover, and diversity.

Ownership

Offer stock options, stock equivalents, and/or have a plan in place to transfer ownership of the company to full-time employees.

Development

+ Subsidize professional development and training for your workers.

+ Distribute an employee handbook.

Wellness

Have an employee committee to monitor and vice on occupational health and safety.

Community

Job Creation

Create job opportunities for chronically underemployed populations, such as at-risk youth, homeless individuals, or individuals who were formerly incarcerated.

Diversity

Have a diverse board of directors, management team, and employee base.

Have suppliers that are majority owned by women or individuals from underrepresented populations.

Giving

Have a formal partnership to support a local charity.

Match charitable contributions made by employees.

Offer incentives for employees to organize service days and/or volunteer activities.

Suppliers

Publicly disclose the social and environmental performance of your suppliers

Local Involvement

Purchase goods and services from local, minority-owned, or woman-owner businesses.

Consider fair trade or environmental standards when making procurement decisions.

Environment

Energy

Purchase renewable energy credits to offset any nonrenewable energy that you use.

Use energy efficient lighting systems, heating and cooling systems, and office equipment.

Environmental Audit

Conduct an annual environmental audit of your energy, water, and waste efficiency. Make the results transparent to the public.

Emissions

Monitor and record your greenhouse gas emissions.

Have a reclamation program to recycle or reuse end products.

Life Cycle

Conduct life cycle assessments of your products.

Have a reclamation program to recycle or reuse end products.

Distribution

Encourage suppliers to start their own environmental reviews or audits, which may cover energy, water, carbon emissions, renewables, or materials.

Choose a legal structure that will help you reach your goals.

Startups can integrate world-positive practices into their business models at the outset by becoming a Benefit Corporation or Certified B Corp.
